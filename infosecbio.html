INFORMATION//SECURITY

What it is. What you do. How to get in.

Information Security — or InfoSec — is the practice of protecting digital assets (data, systems, networks) from unauthorized access, damage, or theft. It is a discipline at the intersection of technology, human behavior, and policy. Every organization that touches a computer has an attack surface. InfoSec professionals are the ones who map it, defend it, and hunt the threats moving across it.

The CIA Triad

C Confidentiality Only authorized parties can read the data. Enforced via encryption, access controls, and least-privilege.
I Integrity Data is accurate and hasn't been tampered with. Enforced via hashing, digital signatures, and audit logs.
A Availability Systems are accessible when needed. Enforced via redundancy, DDoS mitigation, and DR planning.
Attack Surface Every point where an attacker could try to enter or extract data.
Encryption Scrambling data so only parties with the key can read it.
Zero Trust Never trust, always verify — no implicit trust inside or outside the network.
Defense in Depth Layered security so a single failure doesn't expose everything.
// attack vectors

Threat Landscape

  • Phishing Deceptive emails or messages tricking users into surrendering credentials or executing malware.
  • Malware Malicious software — ransomware, trojans, rootkits, spyware — deployed to disrupt or steal.
  • Social Engineering Manipulating humans to bypass technical controls. Often the easiest attack path.
  • Zero-Days Unknown, unpatched vulnerabilities in software exploited before a fix exists.
  • Insider Threats Malicious or negligent employees with legitimate access causing damage from within.
  • Supply Chain Compromising trusted vendors or software updates to reach downstream targets.
  • DDoS Flooding systems with traffic until they are unavailable to legitimate users.
  • SQL Injection Injecting malicious queries into input fields to read or destroy databases.

A Day In The Role

08:00
Shift Handoff & Queue Review
Read the overnight incident log. Prioritize open alerts by severity. Assign tickets.
09:15
Alert Triage
Investigate SIEM alerts — correlate logs, identify false positives, escalate real threats.
11:00
Threat Hunt
Proactively search network telemetry for indicators of compromise (IOCs) and anomalies.
13:30
Incident Response
A live alert: suspected lateral movement. Isolate host, pull forensic artifacts, begin IR runbook.
15:45
Vulnerability Review
Review new CVEs from vendor advisories. Patch prioritization meeting with the IT team.
17:00
Documentation & Reporting
Write up incident findings. Update detection rules in SIEM. Hand off to night shift.
